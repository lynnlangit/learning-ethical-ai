While Federal AI regulation remains in flux, US states have enacted significant legislation targeting "frontier" models, healthcare AI, and consumer transparency.

## üêª California: Transparency in Frontier AI Act (SB 53)

**Status**: Signed Sep 2025 | **Effective**: Jan 1, 2026  
**Scope**: Developers of "Frontier Models" (high compute threshold)

### Key Obligations
- **Safety Frameworks**: Developers must publish safety frameworks outlining risk assessment and mitigation strategies.
- **Incident Reporting**: Mandatory reporting of "critical safety incidents" to the Office of Emergency Services.
- **Cybersecurity**: Implementation of robust cybersecurity practices to prevent model theft or unauthorized access.

> [!NOTE]
> See also **AB 853 (AI Transparency Act)**: Requires detection tools for GenAI systems with >1M users by Aug 2026.

## ü§† Texas: Responsible AI Governance Act (HB 149)

**Status**: Signed June 2025 | **Effective**: Jan 1, 2026  
**Scope**: AI systems used by state agencies & systems interacting with Texas consumers.

### Key Provisions
- **Prohibited Uses**: Bans AI for social scoring, deceptive trade practices, and generating unlawful sexually explicit content.
- **Discrimination**: Prohibits AI development/deployment with intent to unlawfully discriminate.
- **Transparency**: Government agencies must disclose AI interaction to consumers.
- **AI Council**: Establishes a council and a regulatory sandbox for AI innovation.

**Source**: [HB 149 Text (Texas Legislature Online)](https://capitol.texas.gov/BillLookup/History.aspx?LegSess=89R&Bill=HB149)

## üåæ Illinois: Human Rights Act Amendments

**Status**: Enacted 2025  
**Scope**: AI in employment and hiring decisions.

### Key Updates
- **Algorithmic Bias**: Explicitly treats algorithmic discrimination in hiring/promotion as a civil rights violation.
- **Notification**: Employers must notify applicants if AI is used to screen or rank warnings.
